Kathmandu, officially known as Kathmandu Metropolitan City, is the capital and most populous city of Nepal. It is located in the Kathmandu Valley, a large valley in the high plateaus of central Nepal, at an altitude of 1,400 meters (4,600 feet).

Lumbini

Lumbini, is a Buddhist pilgrimage site located in the Rupandehi District of Lumbini Province in Nepal. According to Buddhist tradition, it is the birthplace of Siddhartha Gautama, who later became Shakyamuni Buddha and founded Buddhism.

Chitawan

Chitwan is famous in Nepal for mustard growing and production of mustard oil. This popularity of the mustard in Chitwan is attributed to the predominant soil type silt, resulting from the flooding of the Narayani River and its tributaries.

Ghandruk

Ghandruk is a traditional Gurung village in Kaski Gandaki Province. It is home to a Gurung museum and has one of the best views of Annapurna South, Gangapurna, Annapurna III, Hiunchuli, and Machhapuchhre. There is also a trek from Ghorekhani to Ghandruk that can also be done in one day.
